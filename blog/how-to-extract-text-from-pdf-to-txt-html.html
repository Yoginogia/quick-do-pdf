How to Extract Text from PDF to TXT or fluid HTML

📅 March 2026 · ⏱️ 6 min read · By Quick Do PDF Team

If you've ever tried to copy multiple paragraphs from a two-column PDF document using your mouse, you know the struggle. The formatting breaks, paragraphs merge, and weird special characters appear out of nowhere. In this tutorial, we will show you the exact methods to perfectly extract text from PDFs into clean TXT files, or rich, fluid HTML web pages.

The Problem with Manual Copy-Pasting

PDFs are not designed like Word Documents. They do not understand "paragraphs" or "margins". A PDF simply commands the viewer to "draw the letter A at coordinates X:100, Y:200". When you try to highlight text, the PDF reader desperately attempts to guess where lines end. This leads to broken sentences and missing spaces.

Method 1: Extracting to Plain TXT

If you need raw data for an AI tool, a spreadsheet, or purely to read the contents without formatting, extracting to plain text is the optimal choice.

  2. Upload your document. Our system parses the underlying text matrices (the raw instruction streams) rather than relying on UI rendering.
  3. Download your ultra-lightweight `.txt` file, perfectly sanitized of all fonts, images, and tables.

Method 2: Converting PDF to HTML

What if you want to publish a PDF report directly onto your website for SEO benefits? Search engines struggle to index the contents of heavy, complex PDFs. By converting it to HTML, you make the content accessible, responsive for mobile devices, and easily crawled by Google.

Generate SEO-friendly markup
