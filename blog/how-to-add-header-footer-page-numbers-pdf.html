How to Add Header, Footer & Page Numbers to PDFs

📅 March 2026 · ⏱️ 4 min read · By Quick Do PDF Team

Whether you are presenting a corporate master-report, submitting legal exhibits to court, or formatting an academic thesis, having sequentially numbered pages and unified running headers is an absolute non-negotiable standard. In this guide, we'll show you exactly how to add headers, footers, and Bates numbering to massive PDFs instantly without paying for Adobe Acrobat Pro.

Why Add Headers and Footers to a PDF?

  • Legal Compliance (Bates Numbering): Law firms often strictly require documents to be sequentially labeled with an alphanumeric identifier across thousands of pages.
  • Confidentiality Watermarks: Instantly stamp "CONFIDENTIAL DO NOT DISTRIBUTE" physically onto the top-center of every single page in a 500-page report.
  • Page Numbering: When printing oversized PDFs, page numbers are necessary if physical pages are ever dropped or shuffled.

Using a Batch Processing Web Tool

Manually editing every page using an 'Edit Text' tool would take hours. Our batch-processing tool natively injects the new text elements directly into the PDF content stream across the entire document array mathematically.

Automating Page Number Injection

  1. Launch the Add Header & Footer to PDF utility.
  2. Upload your document into the secure offline workspace.
  3. You'll be presented with text inputs mapped to the document margins: Top-Left, Top-Center, Top-Right, Bottom-Left, Bottom-Center, and Bottom-Right.
  4. Enter your static text (e.g., "Draft V2"). Use the magic template {page} exactly where you want the sequential number to appear (e.g., "Page {page} of {total}").
  5. Hit apply! The system will calculate margins and instantly bake the perfectly aligned text into every single page.
