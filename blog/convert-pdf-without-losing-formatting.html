Attempting to convert a complex, beautiful PDF into an editable Word document often results in total disaster. Headers break from bodies, tables turn into illegible lines of garbled text, and embedded graphics randomly scale out of bounds.

Why Does formatting break during conversion?

A PDF is essentially a digital piece of paper. Text on a PDF isn't aware that it belongs to a "paragraph" or a "table column"; it's just coordinates (X,Y) painted onto a canvas. To turn those disconnected coordinates back into logical Word documents or Excel spreadsheet cells requires highly complex machine-learning recognition algorithms.
