How to Digitally Sign a PDF Document Online

📅 March 2, 2026 · ⏱️ 3 min read · By Quick Do PDF Team

Gone are the days when you had to print a contract, sign it with a pen, scan it back into your computer, and email it. Electronic signatures (e-signatures) have radically simplified administrative workflows. Today, you can legally sign a lease, an invoice, or an employment contract completely online.

Is it Legal to Sign a PDF Electronically?

Yes. The U.S. Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (ESIGN) and the European eIDAS regulation established that electronic signatures hold the same legal weight as traditional handwritten signatures. Adding your digital signature to a form indicates your intent to agree to its contents, which is the core requirement of any binding contract.

How to Sign Your PDF for Free

Using Quick Do PDF, adding a handwritten signature to your digital documentation is quick and keeps your files 100% private.

  1. Open the Sign PDF tool.
  2. Upload the Document: Drag and drop your contract or form into the browser window.
  3. Create Your Signature: You have a choice here. Use your mouse, trackpad, or finger (on touch screens) to physically draw your signature. Alternatively, you can upload a transparent PNG image of your actual penned signature.
  4. Place the Signature: Click anywhere on the PDF page to drop your signature onto the document. You can drag it to the exact line where it belongs and resize it to look natural.
  5. Export: Once everything looks correct, click 'Export Signed PDF'. The document is flattened and downloaded locally to your computer.

💡 Pro Tip: Using a tablet with a stylus or Apple Pencil provides the most realistic looking and precise electronic signature.

A Note on Privacy when Signing Contracts

Be very careful when deciding which tools to use for e-signatures. Contracts inherently contain highly sensitive personal or corporate data. If a tool requires you to upload your contract to their server to sign it, you are trusting them with that data.
